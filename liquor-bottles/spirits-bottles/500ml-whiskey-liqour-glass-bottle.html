Process of Quality Control

 

 

 

1. Measure size of bottles: Use a caliper to measure the dimensions of the bottle, ensuring it meets specified standards.
2. Weight bottle: Place the bottle on a scale to check its weight against predetermined specifications.
3. Measure volume: Fill the bottle with water and pour into a graduated cylinder to determine its capacity.
4. Test print adhesion: Apply tape to printed areas and remove to see if ink sticks, indicating good or poor adhesion.
5. Cut test: Use a tool to cut through the material of the bottle to assess its strength and durability.
6. Spray adhesion test: Spray paint onto the surface and evaluate how well it adheres after drying.
7. Spray adhesion test: After multiple applications, ensure there is no paint left on the tape for excellent adhesion results.
8. Internal pressure detection: Subject the bottle to internal pressure tests to verify its ability to withstand stress without leaking.
9. Vertical axis deviation test: Check alignment by measuring any deviations from vertical orientation when placed in a fixture.
10. Impact resistance detection: Drop the bottle from various heights onto hard surfaces to gauge its impact resistance.
11. Leakage prevention detection: Submerge the bottle underwater while pressurized to identify any leaks or weak points.
