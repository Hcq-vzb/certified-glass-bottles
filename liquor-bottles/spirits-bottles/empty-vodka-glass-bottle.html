Product Introduction:
This 750ml Oslo bottle is made from ultra-clear, high-transparency crystal glass using a single-piece blow-moulding process. The bottle features no mould lines, uniform wall thickness and excellent clarity, perfectly showcasing the wine’s natural colour. Weighing approximately 680–720g, it boasts a sturdy, durable construction with high impact resistance. The neck has a standard 21.5mm Bar Top specification, compatible with a variety of closure options including corks and T-stoppers. With its rounded, stocky Scandinavian minimalist silhouette and low centre of gravity, the bottle offers excellent stability. It is suitable for automated filling lines and facilitates palletised stacking and transport. The large, flat surface area of the bottle body supports customisation through various processes such as sandblasting and screen printing, making it an ideal bottle design for mid-to-high-end spirits such as vodka, gin and flavoured waters.

Design & Quality Control 

 

Design Proposal: Initial bottle concepts are sketched by hand, translating brand identity and market needs into preliminary shape ideas.

 

Modeling: 3D digital models are built based on sketches, fine-tuning bottle proportions, neck specifications, and structural details.

 

Prototyping: 3D-printed physical prototypes are created to validate grip, capacity, and production feasibility.

 

Rendering: High-quality renderings simulate print effects, finishes, and overall visuals for client approval.

 

Printing Handboard: Finished printed samples are produced to verify color accuracy and process quality before mass production.

 

Final Product: The complete custom bottle, including glass, decoration, closure, and packaging, is delivered to the client.

 

Measure size of bottles: Accurate dimensional checks are performed with calipers to ensure neck, body, and base meet specifications.

Weight bottle: Each bottle is weighed on precision scales to verify consistency in glass distribution.

Measure volume: Filling volume is tested to confirm the bottle holds the stated capacity under standard conditions.

Test print adhesion: Print durability is verified by rubbing the decorated surface to check for smudging or peeling.

Cut test: The bottle undergoes controlled cutting to assess glass thickness and structural integrity.

Spray adhesion test: Tape is applied and peeled to test the adhesion of spray coatings.

Spray adhesion test (tape test): After repeated tape pulls, no paint removal confirms strong coating adhesion.

Internal pressure detection: Bottles are pressurized to simulate filling conditions and check for leaks or bursts.

Vertical axis deviation test: Alignment is verified to ensure the bottle stands straight without wobbling.

Impact resistance detection: The bottle is subjected to controlled impact to test its resistance to breakage.

Leakage prevention detection: Sealing performance is tested under pressure to confirm zero leakage.
