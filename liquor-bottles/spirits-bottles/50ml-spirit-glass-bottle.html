Process of Quality Control

1.Measure size of bottles
- Step Description: Use calipers to measure the dimensions of each bottle.
- Purpose: Ensures bottles meet specified size requirements for consistency and functionality.
2.Weight bottle
- Step Description: Place the bottle on a scale to check its weight.
- Purpose: Verifies if the bottle's weight falls within the acceptable range as per standards.
3.Measure volume
- Step Description: Fill the bottle with water and pour it into a graduated cylinder to measure volume.
- Purpose: Confirms the bottle can hold the correct amount of liquid according to its capacity rating.
4.Test print adhesion
- Step Description: Apply printed labels to the bottle surface and assess their adherence over time.
- Purpose: Checks durability and longevity of prints under normal handling conditions.
5.Cut test
- Step Description: Perform cutting tests on sample areas of the bottle material.
- Purpose: Evaluates strength and resistance against sharp objects which could potentially damage the bottle during use.

6.Spray adhesion test
- Step Description: Spray adhesive onto one side of two bottles then press them together; observe separation after drying.
- Purpose: Measures bond strength between surfaces when exposed to moisture or other environmental factors.
7.Spray adhesion test
- Step Description: Similar process as step six but with different types of sprays used for comparison purposes.
- Purpose: Compares performance across various spray formulations to determine best practices for specific applications.
8.Internal pressure detection
- Step Description: Subject the bottle to internal pressure testing equipment.
- Purpose: Assesses ability to withstand pressures encountered during filling processes without leaking or breaking.
9.Vertical axis deviation test
- Step Description: Rotate the bottle around its vertical axis while observing any deviations from straightness.
- Purpose: Identifies potential issues related to structural integrity due to manufacturing defects such as warping or misalignment.
10.Impact resistance detection
- Step Description: Drop the bottle onto hard surfaces at controlled heights and angles.
- Purpose: Determines impact tolerance levels ensuring safety and reliability even after accidental drops.
11.Leakage prevention detection
- Step Description: Seal the bottle completely and immerse it underwater looking for bubbles indicating leaks.
- Purpose: Validates effectiveness of sealing mechanisms preventing leakage throughout storage and transportation phases.
