Label Types

 

Metal Labels: refers to labels made of metal materials, attached to wine bottles or wine packaging, used to display wine-related information, brand logos and decorative purposes. Generally, copper, aluminum, stainless steel and other metals are used as raw materials, and processed through stamping, etching, printing, electroplating and other processes, with unique texture, gloss and durability.

Paper Labels: Paper is the main raw material, and is made through a series of processes such as printing and die-cutting. It is attached to wine bottles or wine packaging and is used to display wine-related information, brand image, and to serve as a decorative label. There are various types of paper used for wine paper labels, the most common of which are coated paper, cardboard, specialty paper, etc. These papers have different textures, glossiness, and strength, and can be selected based on factors such as the brand positioning, style, and cost of the wine. During production, various printing techniques are usually used, such as offset printing, flexographic printing, and gravure printing, etc., to accurately present text, patterns, colors and other elements on the paper to achieve the desired design effect.

Aluminum Labels: They are both beautiful and practical in wine packaging and are widely used in high-end wine, anti-counterfeiting, promotion and information display. They have the advantages of being beautiful, durable, anti-counterfeiting and environmentally friendly.

Pewter Labels:has a vintage and luxurious appearance, making them an exclusive symbol for premium packaging. Customized metal labels Supplier
 

Metal labels are made of aluminum, tin, stainless steel, copper and other metal materials. In the production process, stamping, etching, printing, electroplating and other technologies are used. Stamping can create a three-dimensional shape, etching can achieve fine pattern texture, printing is used to present text and color information, and electroplating can enhance the gloss and texture of the metal surface. Different metals have their own characteristics. Aluminum is light, easy to process, and low in cost; tin has good ductility and unique gloss; stainless steel is corrosion-resistant and durable; copper has a warm texture and a retro charm. These characteristics lay the foundation for the diversified design of wine metal labels.

Custom Paper Labels Supplier

 

Wine paper labels come in a variety of styles, usually chosen based on brand positioning, target audience, and product features. Here is an overview of some common styles:

1. Classic traditional style

Features: Use vintage fonts, badges, family crests, and other elements, commonly seen in high-end wines and whiskeys.

Applicable to: Brands with a long history and emphasis on inheritance.

Elements: Hot stamping, embossing, handwritten fonts, classical borders.

2. Modern minimalist style

Features: Simple typography, geometric graphics, monochrome or two-tone design, highlighting brand names and core information.

Applicable to: Young, fashionable brands, especially craft beer and modern wine.

Elements: Minimalist lines, white space, sans serif fonts.

3. Artistic illustration style

Features: Hand-painted or digital illustrations, with a variety of styles, from abstract to realistic, giving labels a unique personality.

Applicable to: Products that focus on creativity and visual impact, such as craft beer and fruit wine.

Elements: Hand-painted patterns, watercolors, prints, abstract art.

4. Natural and organic style

Features: Use natural elements such as plants, animals, and landscapes, with soft colors to convey the concept of natural and organic.

Applicable to: organic wine, natural wine, and environmentally friendly brands.

Elements: hand-painted plants, earth tones, and environmentally friendly materials.

5. Luxury and high-end style

Features: Use precious metal colors, complex patterns, and high-quality paper to highlight the high-end positioning of the product.

Applicable to: luxury wines, such as high-end champagne and cognac.

Elements: hot stamping, hot silver, embossing, and screen printing.

6. Retro and nostalgic style

Features: Imitate the design of the old times, such as the poster style of the early 20th century, with a sense of nostalgia.

Applicable to: retro brands or limited edition products.

Elements: retro fonts, faded effects, and old-fashioned advertising patterns.
