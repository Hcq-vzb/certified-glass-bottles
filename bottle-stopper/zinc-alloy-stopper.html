Products Technics

Laser engraving

Laser engraving uses high-energy laser beams on zinc alloy stoppers, creating permanent patterns, text or logos via instant high temperatures. It is precise, non-contact, flexible and enhances product recognition.

Polishing

Polishing smooths zinc alloy stoppers via grinding or chemicals, removing surface flaws. It has rough/fine types (fine for mirror finish) and suits complex shapes, boosting luster, safety and coating adhesion for subsequent treatments.

Products Benefits

 

Superior Sealing Performance

Zinc alloy has a dense texture by nature. When combined with sealing components such as silicone rings, it effectively blocks air, moisture, and odors. This creates a stable storage environment for spirits that require long-term preservation, slowing down the deterioration of contents and extending their shelf life.

 

Diverse Design Possibilities

Zinc alloy exhibits excellent casting properties, allowing it to be shaped into intricate forms (such as curved surfaces, patterns, and irregular structures) through processes like die casting. This versatility meets various container design needs, seamlessly adapting to styles ranging from minimalist modern to ornate vintage, thereby enhancing the aesthetic appeal of packaging.

 

Exceptional Durability

Zinc alloy inherently possesses sufficient strength and hardness to resist deformation or breakage. Surface treatments like electroplating and polishing further enhance its corrosion and wear resistance, enabling it to withstand daily impacts, moisture, and other environmental factors, thus ensuring a long service life.

 

Premium Aesthetics and Decorativeness

The metallic material naturally features a delicate luster and texture. Post-treatment finishes, including mirror-like, matte, gold-plated, and chrome-plated effects, significantly elevate the packaging's perceived value, aligning perfectly with high-end products and captivating consumers visually.
