Design & Quality Control

 

 

 

 

 

 

1. Design the bottle cap: The client communicates their requirements with the design team, clarifying the bottle cap's functionality, appearance style, etc. Designers then draw preliminary sketches based on this information, planning basic elements such as dimensions and shape.
2. Modelling: Using professional 3D modelling software, the design sketches are converted into three-dimensional models, accurately presenting the bottle cap's three-dimensional structure and detailed features, facilitating subsequent adjustments.
3. Rendering: Using rendering technology, add materials and lighting effects to the 3D model to generate realistic visual renderings, allowing the client to preview the finished product's appearance.
4. Printing prototype: Create a printing prototype by printing the designed patterns and text onto a physical sample to test the printing quality and colour accuracy, preparing for mass production.
5. Confirmation of cutting die data for bottle caps: Precise measurement of bottle cap dimensions and other data to determine cutting die parameters, ensuring accurate die production and consistent specifications for subsequent bottle cap production.
6.Bottle cap logo design: Design a unique and easily recognisable bottle cap logo based on brand positioning, incorporating creative elements to enhance the brand's visual presence on the bottle cap.
7.Final Product: After optimisation and confirmation through the aforementioned stages, proceed to mass production, strictly controlling quality to produce finished bottle caps that meet design requirements and quality standards.

 

 

 

1. Inspect colour variation: Compare the colour of the bottle caps with the colour chart to check for colour differences and ensure that the colours match the design standards and maintain a consistent visual effect.
2. Inspect appearance: Visually inspect the surface of the bottle caps for scratches, deformities, and defects to ensure that they are smooth and intact and meet quality requirements.
3. Inspect dimensions: Use measuring tools to precisely measure the dimensions of the bottle caps, including diameter and height, to ensure compatibility with the bottle opening and compliance with production specifications.
4. Capping installation test: Simulate actual production conditions to perform bottle cap installation operations, test for smoothness and proper fit, and verify the feasibility of the installation process.
5. Inspect the capping result: After installation, check the position and sealing status of the bottle cap to ensure it is secure and properly aligned, and that the sealing effect meets standards.
6. Sealing test (inverted for 8 hours): Invert the sealed bottle for 8 hours and observe for any leaks to test the sealing performance and ensure the safety of liquid storage.

7. Vacuum Drying Oven: Place the sample in a vacuum drying oven to simulate specific environmental conditions and test the stability and durability of the bottle cap under dry, low-pressure conditions.
8. Bridges Breaking Torque Testing: Using specialized equipment, test the breaking torque of the bottle cap to determine its resistance to damage, ensuring it does not crack during use or transportation.
9.Shake up and down 12 times: Perform a 12-cycle up-and-down shaking test on the bottle body with the cap sealed to inspect the cap's seal integrity and the condition of the liquid inside after shaking, checking for leaks or degradation.
10. Check for foreign objects: Under lampbox illumination, carefully inspect the bottle cap and interior for impurities or foreign objects to ensure product cleanliness and compliance with hygiene standards.
11. Anti-recovery text: Inspect the anti-recovery markings on the bottle cap to confirm clarity and integrity, ensuring its anti-reuse functionality and maintaining product anti-counterfeiting properties.
