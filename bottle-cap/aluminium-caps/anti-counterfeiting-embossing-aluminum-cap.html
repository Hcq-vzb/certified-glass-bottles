Process & Design

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

1. Design the bottle cap: Our R&D team crafts bottle cap designs, combining market trends and client brand identities. We sketch detailed blueprints, considering ergonomics and sealing functionality. This stage lays the foundation for a cap that's both practical and visually appealing.
2. Modelling: Using advanced 3D software, we transform 2D designs into digital models. Engineers refine dimensions, test structural integrity, and simulate real - world use. This virtual stage ensures the cap's design is feasible for production and meets quality standards.
3. Rendering: We create high - fidelity renderings to showcase the cap's final look. These visuals highlight color options, surface textures, and branding potential. Clients get a clear preview, enabling early feedback and design adjustments.
4. Printing prototype: Before mass production, we make printed handboards. Skilled craftsmen apply inks and finishes, mimicking the final product's appearance. This step tests print durability and color accuracy, ensuring consistent branding on caps.
5. Confirmation of cutting die data for bottle caps: A dedicated team verifies cutting die data, ensuring precision in mold creation. We check measurements, notch placements, and material compatibility. Accurate die data prevents production errors, streamlining the manufacturing process.
6.Bottle cap logo design: Our designers develop logo concepts, aligning with the client's brand story. We experiment with fonts, sizes, and embossing effects, ensuring the logo stands out. A well - designed logo elevates brand recognition on the cap.
7.Final Product: After rigorous testing, the final cap rolls out. It embodies design innovation, quality craftsmanship, and brand alignment. Each cap meets global standards, ready to enhance product packaging and consumer experience.

 

1. Inspect colour variation: We use professional color - matching tools to check cap colors. Compare samples against Pantone or custom standards, detecting even subtle hue differences. This ensures consistent branding across batches, avoiding color discrepancies that could impact product perception.
2. Inspect appearance: Trained inspectors examine caps for defects-scratches, deformities, or uneven finishes. We check surface smoothness and logo clarity under bright lighting. Any visual flaw that might affect consumer appeal is identified and addressed here.
3. Inspect dimensions: Precision calipers and gauges measure cap diameters, heights, and thread sizes. We verify adherence to design specs, ensuring compatibility with bottles and capping machinery. Accurate dimensions prevent assembly issues in downstream production.
4. Capping installation test: We simulate real - world capping on sample bottles. Operators use standard equipment to test fit and alignment, checking how caps seat on threads. This step identifies potential jamming or misalignment risks before mass - production capping.​​​​​​​
5. Inspect the capping result: After installation, caps are checked for proper sealing-no gaps, correct torque application. We verify that caps sit flush, with no tilting or incomplete threading. A good capping result ensures leak - proof packaging for end - users.​​​​​​​
6. Sealing test (inverted for 8 hours): Bottles with caps are flipped and left undisturbed for 8 hours. This extreme test checks for slow leaks or seal failures. Any liquid seepage signals a weak seal, preventing poorly sealed products from reaching markets.

7. Vacuum Drying Oven: Caps and sealed bottles go through vacuum oven tests. Controlled heat and pressure simulate transport conditions, testing material durability and seal resilience. This step ensures products withstand temperature and altitude changes during shipping.​​​​​​​
8. Bridges Breaking Torque Testing: Specialized torque tools measure the force needed to break cap "bridges" (security features). We verify torque levels meet anti - tampering standards, ensuring caps stay intact until intended use, enhancing product security.​​​​​​​

11. Anti-recovery text: We verify the presence and clarity of anti - tampering texts . These markings must be legible and unaltered, providing consumers assurance of product integrity and deterring tampering.
