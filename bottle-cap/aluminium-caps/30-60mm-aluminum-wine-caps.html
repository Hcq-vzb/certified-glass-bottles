Process & Design

 

 

 

 

1. Conceptualize the bottle cap shape: Based on functional requirements and brand identity, generate creative ideas and sketches to establish a clear direction for the design.
2. 3D modeling: Utilize modeling software to transform design concepts into precise 3D models, visualizing dimensions, structure, and details for further development.
3. Render visualizations: Apply rendering techniques to produce realistic images, simulating different materials and lighting conditions to support design refinement.
4. Create handmade prototypes: Manually craft initial samples to test tactile feel and structural integrity, providing a tangible reference for mass production.

5. Confirm mold data: Compile and verify essential technical specifications for mold fabrication to ensure dimensional accuracy and consistency in large-scale manufacturing.6. Design brand logo integration: Incorporate the brand logo or other elements into the cap design to enhance visual appeal and strengthen brand recognition.
7. Final product completion: After multiple rounds of testing and optimization, the finished product meets all functional and aesthetic standards and is ready for market launch.

Process of Quality Control

 

 

1. Inspect color variation
Check for any inconsistencies in the color of the bottle cap to ensure uniformity and brand consistency.
2. Inspect the appearance
Examine the overall look and surface finish of the cap for defects such as scratches or blemishes.
3. Inspect the dimensions
Verify that the cap meets the specified size requirements to ensure proper fit and functionality.
4. Capping installation test
Test the installation process of the cap to confirm it can be applied smoothly and securely.
5. Inspect the capping result
Evaluate the outcome of the capping process to ensure the cap is seated correctly and uniformly.
6. Sealing test (inverted for 8 hours)
Invert the capped bottle for 8 hours to check for leaks and confirm the integrity of the seal.

7. Vacuum Drying Oven
Place the cap in a vacuum drying oven to remove moisture and ensure material stability.
8. Bridges Breaking Torque Testing
Measure the torque required to break the tamper-evident bridges to ensure proper security and usability.
9. Shake up and down 12 times
Shake the capped bottle vertically 12 times to test the cap's ability to remain securely in place.
10. Check for foreign objects
Inspect the cap for any foreign particles or contaminants that may affect product safety or quality.
11. Anti-recovery text
Verify that tamper-evident or anti-recovery text is present and legible to prevent unauthorized reuse.
